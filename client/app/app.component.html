Choosing your Race

In ZeldaPlay, your race strongly determines your class. While the ability to allocate skill points to weapons makes it easier to "multi-class", and while classes do not entirely exist, the race you chose to play very much plays a factor into the kind of class you will play to. For example: a Sheikah will almost always play a rogue-like character. Very rarely would you find a Sheikah player charing into battle with a great sword or tower shield expecting to single-handedly destroy the opposing forces. Instead, that would normally be a Goron wielding a hammer.

Note: While your race does not define your class, it very much plays a factor into what kind of character you should probably play. It is a suggestion, not a rule. While you probably will not find the aforementioned Sheikah, it is not entirely impossible.

Allocating your Attribute Points

Your attribute points are your stat points. These help you determine your Strength, Dexterity, and other attributes about your character. To describe each of the attributes, here is an example with tomatoes:

  • Strength:

    The ability to crush a tomato.

  • Dexterity:

    The ability to dodge a tomato.

  • Constitution:

    The ability to eat a bad tomato.

  • Intelligence:

    Knowing that a tomato is a fruit.

  • Wisdom:

    Knowing not to put a tomato in a fruit salad.

  • Charisma:

    Being able to sell a tomato based fruit salad.
Note: If you said that a tomato based fruit salad is just salsa, congratulations, you're a bard.

Assigning your Skills

Your skills are your abilities. In every day life, in combat, in knowledge, magic, thievery: they are your tools of the trade so to speak. Most of the time your skills will play towards your character. Most Gerudo will not have the initial ability to Bluff or Ride, but they will always know how to intimidate anyone they need to and how to handle their weapons. When you create a character you are given 5, 10, 15, or 20 skill points based on a randomly generated number (normally the roll of a d4 die). From there you can put those points into your regular skills, weapon skills, or magic skills. Keep in mind as well that all skills, whether you are trained in them or not, are available to all characters. This just means that a Fairy that wants to use intimidate will have a much harder time than the Gerudo from earlier. If you can't get all the skills you want, don't worry; you gain 10 skill points every level up along with an extra attribute point!

Note: Skill points are shared across Skills, Weapons, and Magic. Spend them wisely.
